About Dordrecht

Dordrecht is a city in the Netherlands, located in the province of South Holland. It is known for its rich history, beautiful architecture, and picturesque canals.

The city has a population of approximately 119,000 people and is the oldest city in Holland, with a history dating back to the 11th century.

Dordrecht is famous for its historic buildings, including the Great Church (Grote Kerk), the Dordrecht Museum, and the many beautiful canals that run through the city.

In addition to its historical significance, Dordrecht is also a vibrant cultural center, with numerous festivals, events, and activities taking place throughout the year.

Whether you're interested in history, culture, or simply enjoying the beautiful scenery, Dordrecht has something to offer everyone.


history

Dordrecht has a rich history that dates back to the 11th century. It was granted city rights in 1220, making it one of the oldest cities in the Netherlands.

During the Middle Ages, Dordrecht became an important trading center.

In the 16th century, Dordrecht played a significant role in the Dutch Revolt against Spanish rule, and it was here that the Union of Dordrecht was signed in 1572, which laid the foundation for the independence of the Netherlands.

Today, Dordrecht is known for its well-preserved historic buildings and canals, which reflect its rich maritime heritage. The city is also home to several museums and cultural institutions that celebrate its history and artistic legacy.

Getting There

Dordrecht is easily accessible by train, bus, and car. The city is located about 20 kilometers from Rotterdam and can be reached in about 30 minutes by train.

For those traveling by car, Dordrecht is located near the A16 motorway, making it easy to reach from other cities in the Netherlands.

Once in Dordrecht, the city is easy to navigate on foot or by bike, with many attractions located within walking distance of each other.
